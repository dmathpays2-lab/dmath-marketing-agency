# Deep Research: Think Energy and Think Plus Program

**Research Date:** March 2026  
**Prepared for:** Agent considering Think Plus Energy Advisor opportunity  
**Disclaimer:** This report is based on publicly available information and should not be considered legal or financial advice.

---

## Executive Summary

### Verdict: **PROCEED WITH EXTREME CAUTION**

Think Energy is a **legitimate Retail Electric Provider (REP)** with proper PUCT licensing, but the Think Plus Program exhibits **classic Multi-Level Marketing (MLM) characteristics** that warrant serious concern.

**Key Red Flags:**
- **Annual subscription fee** ($59 enrollment + $149/year renewal) with no product to sell
- **Multi-tier compensation** requiring downline recruitment for meaningful income
- **Poor customer reviews** across multiple states (1-star ratings for customer service)
- **Free Energy Club discontinued** (June 2025) - major customer acquisition tool removed
- **High churn risk** - electricity customers switch frequently

**Is it legit?** Yes, legally registered.  
**Is it worth doing?** Only if you have a large network AND realistic expectations. Most agents likely lose money.

## 1. Company Structure

### What is Think Energy?
- **Type:** Retail Electric Provider (REP)
- **Parent Company:** Energywell (formerly Elevate)
- **Former Owner:** MP2 Energy (acquired by Shell Energy in 2017)
- **Current Ownership:** Energywell platform (Houston-based energy technology company)
- **Founded:** 2011 (as MP2 Energy's retail brand)
- **PUCT Registration:** Active license as Retail Electric Provider

### Operating Markets
**Texas (ERCOT):**
- Fully deregulated market
- Competes with TXU, Reliant, Direct Energy, Gexa, etc.

**Other States (14 total):**
- Connecticut, Delaware, Illinois, Maine, Maryland
- Massachusetts, New Hampshire, New Jersey, New York
- Ohio, Pennsylvania, Rhode Island, Washington DC

### What is Think Plus?
Think Plus (also called Think+ Network or Think Plus Network) is the **Multi-Level Marketing arm** of Think Energy. It allows independent "Energy Advisors" to:
- Sell Think Energy electricity plans
- Recruit other Energy Advisors
- Earn commissions on personal sales AND downline sales

## 2. Think Plus Program Details

### Is it an MLM? **YES**

The compensation plan exhibits classic MLM structure:

**MLM Characteristics Present:**
- ✅ Annual subscription fee to participate
- ✅ Recruitment-based income tiers (Director, Regional Director, Senior Director, Partner, etc.)
- ✅ Downline commissions (Level 1-10 commissions)
- ✅ "Infinity" bonuses (Coded Infinity Pay, Rank Infinity Pay)
- ✅ Partner Pool (pooled commission sharing)
- ✅ Rank advancement requiring team building

**Why it's not technically a pyramid scheme (legally):**
- There IS a real product (electricity)
- Commissions are primarily tied to actual customer enrollment
- No inventory purchases required

### Compensation Structure

**Personal Income:**
1. **Customer Acquisition Bonuses (CAB)** - One-time payment per customer
   - Residential: $1-$4 per electricity band
   - Commercial: $9-$72 per band
   - Community Solar: $0.50-$1.00 per tier
   - Rooftop Solar: $1,000 + $250 battery bonus

2. **Residual Commissions** - Monthly ongoing payments
   - Residential: $0.20-$4.00/month per customer (depending on usage band)
   - Commercial: $1.80-$14.40/month per customer

3. **Rank Advancement Bonuses** - One-time bonuses for achieving ranks

**Team Income (MLM Component):**
- **Level Commissions:** Earn on up to 10 levels of downline
- **Rank Infinity Pay:** Infinite depth commissions to next equal/higher rank
- **Coded Infinity Pay:** Coded positions (12 per advisor) pay infinitely
- **Partner Pool Pay:** Monthly pool for Partners and above
- **2-Level Mentor Pay:** Bonuses on recruits' customers (being phased out Sept 2025)

### Cost to Join
- **Enrollment Fee:** $59
- **Annual Renewal Fee:** $149
- **Payment Processing:** $0.99 per commission/bonus payment
- **Total First Year:** ~$208 minimum

### Contract Terms
- **Agent Agreement:** Annual auto-renewal
- **No exclusivity:** Can work for other companies
- **Termination:** Can cancel anytime
- **Post-termination:** Commission rights may continue for enrolled customers

## 3. Customer Switching Rules

### Customer Enrollment Terms

**Contract Options:**
- Fixed-rate plans (6, 12, 24, 36 months)
- Variable-rate plans (month-to-month)
- Think Basic (low-cost option, no residuals for pre-Nov 2024 enrollments)
- Think Clean (100% renewable)
- Think ETF Buster (Texas - covers cancellation fees from other providers)

### Costs to Switch TO Think Energy
- **Enrollment Fee:** None
- **Deposit:** May be required based on credit check
  - Poor credit: Deposit typically equal to estimated highest monthly bill
  - Good credit: Often waived
- **Switching Fee:** None (handled by utility/ISO)

### Early Termination Fees (ETF)
- **Fixed-rate plans:** Yes, ETF applies
  - Amount varies by plan (not specified publicly)
  - Can be substantial (reviews mention ~$150+ in some cases)
- **Variable plans:** No ETF

### Customer Cancellation Policy
- **Notice:** 30 days typically required
- **Moving:** ETF may be waived with proof of move outside service territory
- **Contract expiration:** Auto-renews to variable rate if not renewed

### Customer Credit Requirements
- **Soft credit check** performed at enrollment
- **Deposit required** for poor credit or no credit history
- **Prepaid option** not available (Think Energy is post-pay only)

## 4. Marketing Restrictions

### What You CAN Say
- Think Energy offers fixed-rate and variable-rate electricity plans
- Plans include renewable energy options
- Competitive rates in deregulated markets

### What You CANNOT Say (Prohibited Practices)
From Terms & Policies (as of March 2023):

**Prohibited Marketing:**
- ❌ Guaranteed savings claims
- ❌ Misrepresenting relationship with utility companies
- ❌ Implying Think Energy is the utility (they're a supplier)
- ❌ Making income guarantees for prospective agents
- ❌ Using Think Energy trademarks without approval
- ❌ Social media posts that violate PUCT rules
- ❌ Unapproved marketing materials
- ❌ Door-to-door sales (in many markets)
- ❌ Telemarketing without proper compliance

**PUCT Compliance Requirements (Texas):**
- Must provide Electricity Facts Label (EFL) to customers
- Must provide Terms of Service
- Must provide Your Rights as a Customer (YRAC) document
- All marketing materials must be truthful and not misleading
- Cannot disparage competitors

### Approved Marketing Materials
Think Plus provides:
- Branded business cards
- Presentation materials
- Digital assets (for approved use)
- Compliance training

**Website Requirements:**
- Must use approved Think Plus replicated websites OR
- Custom websites require pre-approval
- Must include required disclaimers

### Social Media Rules
- Must disclose material connection (you're a paid advisor)
- Cannot use Think Energy logos without permission
- Must follow FTC endorsement guidelines
- Cannot make income claims in public posts

## 5. Sales Process

### How to Sign Up Customers

**Step 1: Present Options**
- Show available plans for their zip code
- Explain fixed vs. variable rates
- Discuss renewable options

**Step 2: Provide Required Documents**
- ✅ Electricity Facts Label (EFL)
- ✅ Terms of Service
- ✅ Your Rights as a Customer (YRAC)
- ✅ Plan details and pricing

**Step 3: Enrollment**
- Customer completes enrollment online or by phone
- Credit check performed
- Deposit assessed if needed
- Switch initiated (typically 1-7 business days)

**Step 4: Commission Tracking**
- Customer linked to your Energy Advisor ID
- Commission appears in back office
- CAB paid weekly (Friday after enrollment)
- Residuals paid monthly

### Required Documentation
**YOU must have access to:**
- Current EFL for each plan
- Terms of Service
- YRAC document
- PUCT Consumer Protection rules

### Payment Schedule
- **Weekly Payments:** Fridays (CAB, Rank Infinity Bonuses)
- **Monthly Payments:** Residual commissions
- **Payment Method:** Direct deposit or check (processing fee applies)

## 9. Red Flags / Complaints

### BBB Rating
**Not BBB Accredited**
- BBB rating varies by state (not consistently high)
- Pattern of complaints about:
  - Poor customer service
  - Billing issues
  - Rate increases without proper notice
  - Difficulty reaching representatives

### Customer Complaint Patterns

**From MA Energy Ratings, PA Energy Ratings, Texas Electricity Ratings:**

**Consistent Issues:**
1. **Horrible customer service**
   - Phone wait times up to an hour
   - No callbacks despite promises
   - Difficulty reaching live agents

2. **Billing problems**
   - Rates doubled after contract expiration without notice
   - Bills that "look like junk mail"
   - Auto-pay drafting variable rates unexpectedly

3. **Switching difficulties**
   - Accounts blocked from switching to other providers
   - "No man's land" when switching
   - Continued billing after cancellation

4. **Contract renewal issues**
   - No notification of contract expiration
   - Auto-renewal to much higher variable rates
   - From 11¢/kWh to 19¢/kWh without notice (verified complaint)

**Sample Complaints:**
- "Phone wait times can be up to an hour, or they never pick up the phone"
- "They do not return calls or emails"
- "They lie about sending out notices when your contract expires"
- "How do you run a multi billion dollar business with this level of service?"
- "Made a formal complaint with the utility commission and they confirmed they are not following the rules"

### Major Recent Change: Free Energy Club Discontinued
**CRITICAL UPDATE (June 2025):**
- The Free Energy Club (referral program for customers) was **DISCONTINUED**
- This was a major customer acquisition tool
- Customers could previously earn bill credits for referrals
- Now replaced with 9% rebate program (less attractive)
- **Impact on Agents:** Harder to acquire customers through referral incentive

### Agent-Specific Concerns

**Glassdoor/Indeed Reviews Pattern:**
- Mixed reviews at best
- Some report making money with large networks
- Many report difficulty earning enough to cover the annual fee

**Red Flags from Agent Experience:**
1. High annual fee relative to commission per customer
2. Must maintain active status to keep earning
3. Churn rates in electricity market are high (customers switch frequently)
4. Recruiting focus can overshadow product sales

## 10. Realistic Earnings

### The Math

**Customer Acquisition Bonus Example:**
- Average residential customer: ~$2-4 one-time
- Average monthly residual: ~$0.50-2.00/month

**Break-Even Analysis:**
- **Cost:** $208/year ($59 enrollment + $149 renewal)
- **Required customers to break even:**
  - At $2/customer + $0.50/month: ~10-15 customers minimum
  - Must MAINTAIN customers (churn reduces residuals)

**Realistic Scenario:**
- New agent signs up 20 customers in year 1
- Churn rate in electricity: 20-30% annually
- End of year 1: ~14-16 customers remaining
- Year 1 earnings:
  - CAB: $40-80
  - Residuals: ~$100-200
  - **Total: ~$140-280** (minus $208 fees = **-$28 to +$72**)

### What Top Earners Make
- Requires rank advancement to Director+
- Requires building downline team
- Requires 100+ personal customers + team volume
- Top 1% may make $10K+/month
- **Majority likely make little to nothing**

### Comparison to Other REP Agent Programs

| Program | Structure | Annual Fee | Commission |
|---------|-----------|------------|------------|
| Think Plus | MLM | $208 | Low per customer |
| Ambit Energy | MLM | Similar | Similar |
| Ignite (Stream) | MLM | Similar | Similar |
| Traditional Broker | Direct | None | Higher per customer |

**Traditional energy brokers** (not MLM) typically:
- Pay higher per-customer commissions
- Have no annual fees
- Don't require recruitment
- But may require licensing in some states

## Recommended Approach (Conservative Strategy)

### IF You Decide to Proceed

**Phase 1: Validation (Month 1)**
1. **DO NOT pay the enrollment fee yet**
2. Research your local market
   - Are Think Energy rates competitive in YOUR area?
   - Are there cheaper options on Power to Choose?
3. Talk to existing agents (not just the one recruiting you)
4. Ask to see actual commission statements (redacted)

**Phase 2: Limited Trial (Months 2-3)**
1. Enroll ONLY if you have a warm list of 50+ prospects
2. Focus on **personal sales only** initially
3. Set a goal: 20 customers in 90 days or quit
4. Do NOT recruit others yet
5. Track your actual time investment vs. earnings

**Phase 3: Evaluate (Month 4)**
1. Calculate your true hourly wage
2. Calculate customer retention rate
3. Decide: Continue, Pivot to recruiting, or Quit

### DO NOT Do These Things

- ❌ Do not recruit others until you've made money yourself
- ❌ Do not make income guarantees to prospects
- ❌ Do not spend money on paid advertising initially
- ❌ Do not quit your day job for this
- ❌ Do not pressure friends/family
- ❌ Do not misrepresent rates or savings

### Better Alternatives to Consider

1. **Licensed Energy Broker**
   - Higher commissions
   - Can offer multiple providers
   - No MLM structure
   - May require state licensing

2. **Solar Sales**
   - Higher ticket items
   - $1,000+ per sale possible

   - Similar skills required

3. **Direct Sales for Established REP**

## Final Verdict

### Should You Join Think Plus?

**Consider it IF:**
- You have a large network (500+ contacts)
- You understand and accept the MLM model
- You're willing to recruit others (not just sell electricity)
- You can afford to lose $208 if it doesn't work out
- You have experience in network marketing
- You believe in the renewable energy mission

**Avoid it IF:**
- You're uncomfortable with MLM/recruiting
- You expect immediate income
- You don't have a large warm market
- You need guaranteed income
- You're not willing to invest time in team building
- You expect this to replace a full-time job quickly

### The Bottom Line

Think Plus is a **legitimate but challenging opportunity**. The electricity market is highly competitive, the company has customer service issues, and the MLM structure means most participants will lose money.

**Success requires:**
- Treating it like a business, not a side hustle
- Large personal network
- Willingness to recruit actively
- Thick skin for rejection
- Financial runway to survive the first year

**Realistic expectation:** If you work 10-20 hours/week consistently for 6 months, you might build a $500-2,000/month income. Most people will earn $0-100/month or lose money.
