# MCA Lead Generation: Pay-After-Funding & Performance-Based Models

*Research compiled: March 17, 2026*

---

## Executive Summary

This research covers merchant cash advance (MCA) lead generation companies that operate on "pay after funding" or performance-based payment models, as opposed to upfront lead purchase models. The MCA industry has various partnership structures ranging from pure commission splits to revenue sharing and white-label arrangements.

---

## 1. Pay-Per-Funded-Deal Models

### How They Work

Pay-per-funded-deal (PPFD) models are performance-based arrangements where lead generation companies or referral partners only get paid when a deal actually funds. This shifts risk from the broker/funder to the lead provider.

### Commission Structures

| Model Type | Typical Split | Who Pays |
|------------|---------------|----------|
| **Percentage of Commission** | 30-50% of YOUR commission | Lead gen takes cut from broker's earnings |
| **Flat Fee per Funded Deal** | $500-$5,000+ depending on deal size | Broker pays fixed amount per funded deal |
| **Percentage of Funded Amount** | 1-5% of total funded amount | Deducted from proceeds or paid by funder |
| **Hybrid** | Lower upfront + success fee | Combination model |

### Key Considerations

**Taking a Cut of YOUR Commission vs. Charging Separately:**
- Most PPFD models take a percentage **from your commission** (not the funder's side)
- Example: If you earn 10% commission on a $100,000 deal ($10,000), a 50/50 split means you keep $5,000
- Some funders have direct referral programs where they pay the referrer separately (better for you)

**Typical Splits:**
- **50/50 Split**: Common for warm referrals with some pre-qualification
- **70/30 Split** (you keep 70%): Common when you do most of the work
- **80/20 Split**: Rare, usually for high-volume partners

### Companies Offering PPFD Models

Based on research, very few pure "pay after funding" lead gen companies exist. Most require upfront payment. However, some partnership models include:

- **ISO Partnerships** (see Section 2)
- **Funder Direct Referral Programs** (see Section 5)
- **Performance Marketing Agencies** (rare in MCA space)

**⚠️ Reality Check:** Most MCA lead providers (Synergy Direct, Vellko Media, etc.) charge upfront for leads ($1-40 per lead) and do NOT offer pure pay-after-funding models.

## 2. Referral Partnerships & ISO Relationships

### What is an ISO?

An **Independent Sales Organization (ISO)** is a third-party company that sells payment processing and MCA services on behalf of funders. ISOs are independent contractors, not employees.

### How ISO Partnerships Work

1. **You source the merchant** → Submit to funder through ISO channel
2. **Funder approves and funds** → Commission is paid to ISO
3. **ISO splits commission** with you according to your agreement

### Commission Structures

| Split Type | Typical Range | Notes |
|------------|---------------|-------|
| **50/50 Split** | 50% to ISO, 50% to you | Common for new relationships |
| **60/40 Split** | 60% to you, 40% to ISO | After proving volume |
| **70/30 Split** | 70% to you, 30% to ISO | High volume producers |
| **80/20 Split** | 80% to you, 20% to ISO | Exceptional volume only |

### Residual Income Model

Many ISOs offer **residual income** on payment processing:
- You earn a percentage of processing volume every month
- Continues as long as the merchant processes through the ISO
- Typical: 0.1-0.3% of monthly processing volume

### MCA-Specific ISO Commission

For direct MCA deals, ISOs typically earn:
- **5-15% of the funded amount** as their commission from the funder
- They then split this with sub-brokers/referrers

Example on $100,000 MCA:
- Funder pays ISO: $10,000 (10%)
- ISO keeps: $3,000 (30%)
- You receive: $7,000 (70%)

## 3. Legitimacy Checks: Red Flags & Scams

### Major Red Flags in Lead Gen Companies

🚩 **1. Guaranteed Funding Promises**
- No one can guarantee funding
- Legitimate providers qualify leads, not approve them

🚩 **2. No Physical Address or Verified Business Registration**
- Check for real business license
- Verify on state registration databases
- Look for professional website (not Gmail/Yahoo emails)

🚩 **3. Requesting Upfront Fees for "Exclusive" Leads**
- Be wary of high upfront costs without refund policy
- Check if they offer any performance guarantee

🚩 **4. Oversold/UCC Leads**
- UCC leads are the most "beat up" in the industry
- These businesses receive constant calls
- Low conversion rates

🚩 **5. No Return/Refund Policy**
- Legitimate providers offer 10-25% return allowance
- Synergy Direct, for example, allows up to 25% returns

🚩 **6. Pressure Tactics & Urgency**
- "Act now or miss out"
- Pushing for immediate payment
- Not allowing time for due diligence

🚩 **7. Too-Good-To-Be-True Pricing**
- Free leads that waste your time
- Prices 40%+ below market (indicates poor quality)

### Verifying Legitimacy

**Step 1: Check Business Registration**
- Verify company exists in state records
- Check how long they've been in business
- Look for complaints on BBB, Trustpilot

**Step 2: Ask for References**
- Request 3+ current clients you can contact
- Ask about conversion rates and ROI
- Check online reviews from other brokers

**Step 3: Start Small**
- Never place large orders with new providers
- Test with minimum order first
- Track your conversion metrics

**Step 4: Check Communication**
- Professional email domain (not Gmail/Yahoo)
- Willing to do video calls
- Provides clear contract terms

### MCA-Specific Scams to Watch For

| Scam Type | How It Works | Protection |
|-----------|--------------|------------|
| **Fake MCA Broker** | Uses stolen identities, copies real broker websites | Verify credentials, check references |
| **Phishing for Documents** | Requests extensive docs, then applies for loans themselves | Never send sensitive docs over unsecured email |
| **Hidden Fee Scams** | Obscure true costs until after signing | Read all terms, get everything in writing |
| **Confession of Judgment (COJ) Abuse** | Uses COJs to get judgments without notice | Avoid COJs entirely if possible |
| **Stacking Schemes** | Pushes multiple MCAs simultaneously | Never stack without clear disclosure |

### Predatory Contract Terms

⚠️ **Watch for these clauses:**

1. **Exclusive Territory Clauses** - May limit your ability to work with others
2. **Non-Compete Agreements** - Could restrict your future business
3. **Confession of Judgment (COJ)** - Allows them to obtain judgment without trial
4. **Personal Guarantees** - Makes you personally liable for business debts
5. **Jurisdiction Clauses** - Forces litigation in distant states
6. **Automatic Renewal** - Contracts that renew without explicit consent
7. **Excessive Early Termination Fees** - High penalties for leaving

## 4. Alternative Models

### Revenue Share (Rev Share)

**How it works:**
- Partner receives percentage of ongoing revenue from funded deals
- Not just upfront commission
- Aligns interests for long-term success

**Typical Structure:**
- 10-20% of the broker's commission on deal
- Plus potential residuals on renewals

**Best for:** Partners who can bring repeat business

### Commission Splits

**Traditional Split Model:**
| Role | Typical Split |
|------|---------------|
| Lead Generator | 20-30% |
| ISO | 20-30% |
| Closer/Broker | 40-60% |

**Tiered Commission:**
- Higher splits for higher volume
- Example: 50/50 up to $100k, then 60/40, then 70/30

### White Label Model

**How it works:**
- You appear as the funding company
- Behind the scenes, another funder provides capital
- They handle underwriting, you handle sales

**Typical Split:**
- You keep: 30-50% of commission
- White label provider keeps: 50-70%

**Pros:**
- Build your own brand
- Higher perceived authority
- Can charge premium rates

**Cons:**
- Less control over underwriting
- Reputation risk if funder performs poorly
- May require minimum volume commitments

### Hybrid Models

**Example Hybrid Structure:**
- Small monthly retainer for lead access ($500-$2,000)
- Reduced per-lead cost
- Bonus/override on funded deals
- Revenue share on renewals

**Best for:** Established brokers with predictable volume

## 6. Summary & Recommendations

### The Reality of "Pay After Funding" Models

**Hard Truth:** True "pay after funding" lead generation companies are **rare** in the MCA industry. Most lead providers charge upfront because:
1. They incur costs generating leads
2. They can't control your sales process
3. Many brokers don't convert leads effectively

### Better Alternatives to Pure PPFD

1. **Funder Direct Referral Programs**
   - Lendio, NewCo, etc. pay when deals fund
   - No upfront cost to you
   - Established infrastructure

2. **ISO Partnerships**
   - Better splits than lead buying
   - Access to multiple funders
   - Training and support often included

3. **Start with Aged/Cheap Leads**
   - Test your process at low cost
   - Scale up as you prove conversion
   - Many providers at $0.01-$0.05 per lead

### What to Negotiate

If you do work with lead providers:
- **Return policy** (aim for 20-25% return allowance)
- **Credit for bad leads** (disconnected numbers, wrong info)
- **Exclusivity** (are leads sold to multiple brokers?)
- **Replacement policy** (what happens with bad leads?)

### Final Verdict

| Model | Risk Level | Upfront Cost | Best For |
|-------|------------|--------------|----------|
| **Funder Referral** | Low | None | Most brokers |
| **ISO Partnership** | Low-Medium | None/Minimal | Brokers with some experience |
| **Buy Aged Leads** | Medium | Low ($0.01-$0.05/lead) | Testing/Training |
| **Buy Live Transfers** | High | High ($30-$40/lead) | Experienced closers |
| **White Label** | High | Medium-High | Established brokers |
