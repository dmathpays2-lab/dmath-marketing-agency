## Part 1: Company Profile - Think Plus Energy

### Company Overview

**Company Name:** Think Energy (also operating as Think Plus / Think+)

**Business Type:** Retail Electricity Provider (REP)  
**Markets Served:** Deregulated electricity markets (primarily Texas)  
**Website:** [ThinkEnergy.com](https://www.thinkenergy.com)

### What is Think Plus Energy?

Think Plus is the **independent consultant/agent program** for Think Energy, allowing individuals to earn commissions by referring customers to Think Energy's electricity plans. This is a legitimate multi-level marketing (MLM) structure operating within the regulated energy sector.

**Key Distinction:**
- **Think Energy** = The retail electricity provider that sells electricity plans
- **Think+ (Think Plus)** = The independent consultant program for customer acquisition

## Part 2: Energy Sector Marketing Regulations

### Why Energy Marketing is Heavily Regulated

The energy sector faces strict marketing regulations due to:

1. **Consumer Protection:** Electricity is an essential service; predatory practices harm vulnerable populations
2. **Historical Abuse:** Aggressive door-to-door tactics and misleading rate advertising
3. **Public Safety:** Misinformation about electricity services can create safety risks
4. **Market Integrity:** Ensuring fair competition in deregulated markets

### Key Regulatory Frameworks

#### 1. TCPA (Telephone Consumer Protection Act)

**Restrictions:**
- ❌ **NO cold calling** without prior express written consent
- ❌ **NO robocalls** to cell phones without consent
- ❌ **NO autodialed calls** to numbers on Do Not Call registry
- ✅ **ALLOWED:** Manual calls to existing customers or those with express consent

**Penalties:** $500-$1,500 per violation

#### 2. National Do Not Call Registry

**Requirements:**
- Must scrub call lists against DNC database every 31 days
- Cannot call numbers listed for 31+ days
- Existing business relationship exemption (18 months from last transaction)
- Written inquiry exemption (3 months from inquiry)

#### 3. State-Specific Regulations (Texas PUCT)

**Texas Public Utility Commission Rules:**

| Rule | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| **Certificate of Registration** | All REPs must register with PUCT |
| **Electricity Facts Label (EFL)** | Must provide standardized plan disclosures |
| **Terms of Service** | Written contract terms required |
| **Your Rights as a Customer** | Specific disclosure document required |
| **Marketing Material Review** | Certain materials require pre-approval |

**Door-to-Door Restrictions in Texas:**
- Must display company identification
- Cannot use deceptive practices
- Must honor "Do Not Knock" registry (where applicable)
- Local ordinances may require permits

#### 4. Required Disclosures

All marketing must include or link to:
- Electricity Facts Label (EFL)
- Terms of Service
- Your Rights as a Customer (YRAC)
- Base charge, energy charge, and TDU delivery charges
- Early termination fees
- Contract length
- Renewable content percentage

#### 5. CAN-SPAM Act (Email Marketing)

**Requirements:**
- Accurate "From" information
- Truthful subject lines
- Clear identification as advertisement
- Physical address included
- Working unsubscribe mechanism
- Honor opt-out requests within 10 business days
